All 7 themes pass WCAG AAA for both normal and large text.

Piperly (15.3:1) — Best contrast. Uses the app’s own dark palette. Seamless transition from app to reader.
Sunshine (14.3:1) — Excellent. Happy orange-tinted background, clearly distinct from Cozy’s muted sepia.
Ocean (10.3:1) — Excellent. Light blue with deep navy text. High contrast, calm feel.
Lavender (9.5:1) — Excellent. Light purple with deep violet text. Strong contrast.
Night Owl (14.7:1) — Near-black with light gray. Very similar to Piperly but uses different hex values (#1C1C1E/#E0E0E0 vs #1C1C2E/#E8E8F0).
Forest (7.3:1) — Good. Just above the AAA threshold. Lowest-contrast light theme, but still excellent.
Cozy (7.1:1) — Good. Sepia tone with brown text. Just above AAA threshold. Classic ebook feel.

Notes:
• Piperly vs Night Owl: These are very similar visually (#1C1C2E vs #1C1C1E). You may want to keep just one dark theme, or keep both if the subtle difference matters.
• Forest and Cozy are the tightest at ~7:1. Still AAA, but darkening the text slightly would add headroom.
• These themes only affect the reader view (EPUB content via Readium preferences). The app chrome stays on the existing dark palette.
• All themes use large text (1.8x = ~23px rendered) with high line-height (2.0) and extra word-spacing — optimized for early readers ages 8-12.
